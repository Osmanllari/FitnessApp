Back Workouts

Back Extension
Back extension exercises can strengthen lower back muscles. This includes the erector spinae, which supports the lower spine.

Deadlift
Deadlifting can increase core strength, core stability and improve your posture. Deadlifting trains most of the muscles in the legs, lower back and core.

Pull Ups
A pullup is an upper body strength training exercise. To perform a pullup, you start by hanging onto a pullup bar with your palms facing away from you and your body extended fully.

Bent-Over Row
Bent-over rows can help build a stronger back. The bent-over row is an effective back exercise that activates both upper-back muscles and lower-back muscles.

Chest Supported Row
As the name suggests, it's a row where your chest is supported. By leaning into a bench with your upper torso, you're removing the need for your back to stabilise the movement.

Lat Pulldown
The lat pulldown is a fantastic exercise to strengthen the latissimus dorsi muscle, the broadest muscle in your back, which promotes good postures and spinal stability.
